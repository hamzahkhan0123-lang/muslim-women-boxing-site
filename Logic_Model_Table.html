Inclusive Boxing Program: Strategic Plan

Issue Statement

Muslim women in Birmingham face significant barriers to sport participation due to cultural norms, lack of female-only spaces, and limited representation in local clubs. Sport England data (2023) shows that only 18% of Muslim women meet recommended physical activity levels compared to 32% of the general female population. This initiative aims to tackle these inequalities by creating a culturally sensitive, female-only boxing program that promotes health, confidence, and social inclusion.

Structures, Agencies and Policies

The program aligns with Sport England’s Uniting the Movement strategy and the Equality Act 2010, which mandate inclusive opportunities. Delivery will involve local Active Partnerships, Birmingham City Council, and community organisations to ensure safe, culturally appropriate provision. Funding is supported through Sport England’s Tackling Inequalities Fund.

Delivery Plan

  • Target Group: Muslim women aged 18–40 in Birmingham
  • Setting: Female-only community sports hall
  • Partners: Local mosques, Birmingham City Council, Active Partnerships
  • Safeguarding: DBS-checked female coaches, privacy screens, cultural sensitivity training
  • Risk Mitigation: Safe transport options, emergency procedures, insurance cover

Monitoring and Evaluation

Data will be collected through attendance logs, pre/post confidence surveys, and physical activity diaries. Feedback will be analysed monthly to adapt sessions and improve retention. A final report will summarise outcomes against targets and inform future funding bids.
