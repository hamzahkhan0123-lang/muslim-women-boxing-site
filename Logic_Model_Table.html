Logic Model for Inclusive Boxing Program

Inputs Activities Outputs Short-Term Outcomes Long-Term Impact Evaluation Methods Assumptions & External Factors
Funding (£12,000), venue, culturally sensitive coaches, boxing equipment Community engagement (Weeks 1–2), coach training (Weeks 3–4), inclusive boxing sessions (Weeks 5–16) 2 trained coaches, 3 weekly sessions for 12 weeks, 30 participants enrolled Increase physical activity by 20%, retention rate of 80%, improved confidence scores in post-program survey Sustained participation among Muslim women, greater representation in local sport, improved health and social inclusion Attendance tracking, pre/post confidence surveys, physical activity logs, feedback forms Community support, cultural acceptance, availability of female coaches, stable funding, safe transport for participants

This logic model aligns with Sport England’s Uniting the Movement strategy by tackling inequalities in participation. Activities such as female-only sessions and culturally sensitive coaching address intersectional barriers (gender, religion, ethnicity) and issues of social exclusion. Outcomes will be monitored through attendance data and confidence surveys, aiming for a 20% increase in weekly physical activity and an 80% retention rate after 12 weeks.
