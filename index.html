How Religious Beliefs Can Affect Participation in Sports – And How to Make Sports More Inclusive

Introduction and Aims

This project explores how religious beliefs, particularly within Islam, influence participation in sports. It aims to identify barriers and propose inclusive strategies that respect religious values while promoting active engagement.

Context

  • Modesty requirements (e.g., hijab, long sleeves)
  • Gender segregation in physical activities
  • Lack of representation (few Muslim female coaches)
  • Example: Muslim Women Boxing Initiative – empowering Muslim women through culturally sensitive boxing programs

Critical Theory and Sport

Critical theory helps examine power structures and cultural norms in sport. It promotes agency—the ability to act independently and make choices—especially for marginalized groups.

  • Challenges dominant ideologies
  • Focuses on empowerment and cultural transformation
  • More agency-oriented than functionalism or conflict theory

Agency

Agency refers to the capacity of individuals to act independently. In sport, it means:

  • Muslim women choosing to participate despite societal pressures
  • Creating inclusive spaces and programs
  • Having Muslim female coaches who advocate for their communities

Major Tenets of Critical Theory in Sport

  • Power and inequality in sport
  • Cultural hegemony and marginalization
  • Resistance and social change
  • Sport as a space for identity and interaction

Beyond Exploitation

Critical theory moves beyond seeing athletes as exploited labor. It views sport as a site for resistance, expression, and transformation.

Shaped by Interactions / Shaping Wider Culture

Sport is both influenced by and influences culture. Inclusive practices can reshape societal views on Muslim women in sport.

Potential for Change

  • Policy reforms (e.g., allowing religious attire)
  • Community initiatives (e.g., boxing programs)
  • Representation and leadership (e.g., Muslim female coaches)
