Muslim Women Boxing Initiative – Breaking Barriers in Sport

Exploring how beliefs—particularly within Islam—shape participation in sport. Identifying barriers and showcasing strategies that respect faith, modesty, and community while increasing access and agency.

The Issue: Barriers to Muslim Women’s Participation in Sport

Muslim women in the UK face cultural and religious barriers to sport participation, including modesty requirements, gender segregation, and lack of representation. Evidence: Only 18% of Muslim women meet recommended physical activity levels (Sport England, 2023). This initiative aims to break these barriers through inclusive boxing programmes.

Delivery Plan

  • Target group: Muslim women aged 16–30 in Birmingham.
  • Setting: Female-only boxing sessions in community gym.
  • Partners: Local mosques, England Boxing, Sport England.
  • Safeguarding: DBS checks, female coaches, modest attire policy.
  • Risks: Low uptake → mitigated by community ambassadors.
